Trip #3 – AirBNB Edition, Roanoke IN

2024-04-08

Trip #3 to Ohio went off without a hitch! Changed it up a little this time and went with an AirBNB instead of a tent—the kids appreciated the wi-fi and running water. Less appreciated was their dad tormenting them with gen-z slang..

Trading Nylon for Drywall

Trading nylon walls for drywall was the move. The kids and I posted up in a roomy AirBNB on the edge of Roanoke where the April grass was greening fast enough to squeak under your shoes. A massive backyard—complete with creaky old swing set—meant every lull in screen time turned into barefoot sprints and improvised tag tournaments.

Comfort Level: Legendary

  • Beds: Pillow-top, cloud-soft, and allegedly “better than Mom’s couch,” according to Rowan.
  • Wi-Fi: Fiber so quick the speed test broke triple digits. Even Xander, a notorious latency snob, had zero notes.
  • Running water: Endless hot showers = no more campground coin-op roulette.

Aunt Maire Came Through

Saturday, Maire cruised in for the afternoon. We caught up on the porch while the kids roamed the yard, and we somehow squeezed five rounds of “tell-me-everything” into two hours. Sunshine, sibling laughs, and freshly cut grass—you could bottle that vibe as antidepressant.

Xander’s CoD Lab

Inside, Xander went full mad-scientist on the gaming laptop, tuning “the perfect” Call of Duty loadout. Every fifteen minutes he emerged to announce a new recoil pattern breakthrough, then vanished again into the digital war zone. Proud Dad moment? Absolutely—though I’ll still get smoked next match.

Plex Envy Is Real

Look, I’m smug about my own Plex server … but this AirBNB host? They had a catalog that straight-up humbled me: vintage anime, remastered deep-cut sci-fi, Criterion classics I’ve been hunting for ages. I bowed in silent respect—then mentally budgeted for a few more hard drives.

Food Highlights

Local pizza that slapped: The Patio Pizza & BBQ on High Street—crispy-edge thin crust, smoked wings, ranch that could start wars.

Epic breakfasts: Dad’s skillet hash (hash browns, farm-stand eggs, THICK bacon). When the syrup bottle ran empty, the kids scraped the last drops with forks—call that a five-star review.

Dad-Level Cringe, Updated

With creature comforts handled, I leaned into my true role: tormentor-via-slang. Phrases like “That pizza, low-key bussin’, no cap” and “Y’all vibin’ or nah?” echoed through the halls. Groans achieved a perfect chorus. Goal met.

Wrap-Up

No tent poles bent, no rainfly sagged, no one melting down at 3 a.m.—just warm beds, fast internet, and backyard laughter I’ll replay on the hard drives of the heart. The kids rated the trip “9.7/10—minus 0.3 for Dad’s vocabulary.” I can live with that.
