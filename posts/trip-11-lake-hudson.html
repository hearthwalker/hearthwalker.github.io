Kid Trip #11 -- April 2026

Some trips go exactly as planned. This one went better.

Kid Trip #11 was the first of the warm months' tradition -- loading up Hearthwalker and making the 800-mile run from Albion, Nebraska to southwest Michigan to spend a long weekend camping with my four kids. It won't always be easy. This one certainly wasn't. But it was absolutely worth it.

The Drive

Left Albion at 5:30 Wednesday evening with a van full of gear, a cold Mountain Dew and a sky that looked like it was putting on a show just for the occasion. Pink thunderheads lit up the plains all the way across Nebraska and into Iowa -- the kind of sky that makes you remember why you drive instead of fly.

Fueled in Moscow, Iowa, grabbed a few hours of sleep at a rest area and rolled into Hicksville, Ohio at 4pm Thursday to pick up the crew. Four kids buckled in, chaos initiated, Michigan bound.

The Campsite

Lake Hudson State Recreation Area sits just north of the Ohio border in Lenawee County -- a quiet, wooded dark sky preserve wrapped around a no-wake lake. It's the kind of place that doesn't advertise itself. You have to know to look.

We arrived to find our site thoroughly acquainted with three days of April rain. Three inches of mud, wall to wall. The kids didn't notice. Within twenty minutes they were filthy, delighted, and completely at home.

The 10x10 shelter went up. The Coleman Skylodge claimed its ground. Hot dogs went on the fire. Orange sodas cracked open all around. Trip #11 was officially underway.

The Plano Box

My grandfather carried this tackle box for decades. It came to me when he passed, and it's been waiting for the right moment ever since. This was it.

I set it on the picnic table Friday morning and opened it up. Four kids went quiet for approximately one second before absolute chaos erupted in the best possible way. Every lure examined. Every compartment raided. Questions fired faster than I could answer them.

By the end of the morning all three boys had caught bluegill. First fish on grandpa's gear. That box has a new chapter now.

The Weekend

Muddy. Loud. Perfect.

Kids fished until their arms were tired. Someone -- I won't say who -- attempted to eat a bluegill directly from the hook. The fire burned every night. S'mores happened in quantities I won't disclose. The little one wrapped herself in a blanket and declared the tent "the best house ever."

It rained. It cleared. It rained again. We didn't care.

The Drive Home

They chattered the whole way back to Hicksville about next month's trip. Before I'd even crossed back into Indiana they were already planning what to pack.

That's the whole thing right there.

Trip #11. Many more to come. 🚐
