Trip #10 -- Independence Dam SP, OH

2025-09-06

Ohio Trip #10 -- September 2025

Some trips earn their place in the mythology. This one just earned a quiet corner of my heart.

Independence Dam sits on the Maumee River outside Defiance, Ohio -- still the coolest city name in the Midwest, I don't make the rules. We've camped here before on Trips #4, #5, and #8, and by now the place feels less like a destination and more like a familiar room in the house. We rolled in, unloaded, and the kids went feral in the good way -- that immediate release you see when they know the rules are softer here.

The weather held. Nothing broke. Nobody cried. A weekend with zero drama is its own quiet miracle.

Hoodies On, Fire Up

September in Ohio walks a fine line. Days stay warm, but evenings carry a cool edge -- the first whisper that autumn is coming. The maples along the river were just starting to turn, amber at the tips. Hoodies came out at sundown. String lights went up around the tent. Firewood split clean.

Xander worked a piece of kindling with the Gerber, focused the way he gets when a task deserves his full attention. Rory turned up with a pink pool-noodle lightsaber and declared war on invisible enemies. Dawson cooked breakfast sausages over the fire the next morning -- held them up three-to-a-skewer like a trophy. Khai took the slow, thoughtful approach to everything and somehow never missed a beat.

The Chicken Incident

Not every meal was a win. I put chicken thighs over the fire, got distracted, overcorrected, and ended up with a tray of charcoal briquettes in the vague shape of poultry. Documentation is thin on this one -- the photographer was busy juggling hot metal and producing some artisanal dad-vocabulary. The chicken got yeeted into the woods. The raccoons ate well that night. We pivoted to smoked sausage and peppers in the skillet, and nobody complained.

Cards by Lantern Light

The best part might have been the quietest part. Picnic table after dark. Lantern humming. A deck going around. Rory yawning mid-hand, refusing to go to bed because she didn't want to miss anything. Dawson dealing with the concentration of a riverboat gambler. No phones for a stretch of time I didn't bother to measure.

That's the whole trip. Fresh air. Time together. A fire that burned low and then lower. Quiet joy in good chairs with good kids.

Trip #10. Simple. Good. 🍂
